Program Glossary

Energy Programs & Tools

Federal programs and tools administered or supported by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and USDA that define incentives, modeling requirements, and documentation standards.

🌱 Iowa’s New IRA Energy Rebates (2026)
Helping homeowners cut energy costs with performance‑based upgrades and electrification incentives

Iowa is rolling out two major Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) rebate programs in 2026, administered by the Iowa Economic Development Authority (IEDA). JYA Inc. helps homeowners and agencies navigate these programs from assessment through installation.

🏠 HOMES Program — Rebates for Measured Energy Savings
For all income levels. Rewards whole home efficiency improvements that achieve 20%+ energy savings. Rebates up to $4,000, or up to $8,000 for LMI households. Applies to insulation, air sealing, duct sealing, and high efficiency HVAC upgrades. Requires energy modeling or utility bill based savings verification.

⚡ HEEHR Program — Instant Rebates for Electrification
For Low to Moderate Income (LMI) households. Point of sale discounts for heat pumps, heat pump water heaters, electric stoves, panel upgrades, wiring, and insulation. Up to $14,000 per household. Income based coverage: <80% AMI up to 100% of equipment cost; 80–150% AMI up to 50% of cost.

Key Rules for 2026
No double dipping: HOMES and HEEHR cannot be used on the same measure. State certified contractors required. Can be combined with the 25C Federal Tax Credit (30% up to $2,000 for heat pumps).

JYA Inc. provides NEAT modeling preparation, contractor coordination, and rebate ready documentation for Iowa homeowners and agencies.

HOMES
Whole‑Home Savings
Home Owner Managing Energy Savings (HOMES) — A U.S. DOE rebate program providing incentives for whole home energy upgrades based on measured or modeled energy savings.
HEAR
Assessment Rebates
Home Energy Assessment Rebate (HEAR) — A DOE program offering rebates for home energy assessments and targeted efficiency improvements, including electrification and weatherization.
NEAT
DOE Modeling
National Energy Audit Tool (NEAT) — DOE modeling software used to calculate baseline energy use, evaluate retrofit options, and determine cost effective measures. Weatherization Assistant includes NEAT and supports state ready documentation.
REAP
Rural Funding
USDA Rural Energy for America Program (REAP) — Federal grant and loan program supporting energy efficiency upgrades and renewable energy projects for rural small businesses and agricultural producers.
